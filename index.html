Hiring and Working in Web3 Is Broken

Web3 talent is everywhere, yet hiring remains inefficient. Projects rely on timelines, Discord servers, and engagement posts to find workers, often attracting unqualified applicants, scammers, or people not ready to execute.

On the other side, skilled Web3 professionals struggle to find legitimate, paid opportunities. Jobs are scattered, unclear, underfunded, or disappear without accountability. The result is wasted time, broken trust, and stalled growth for both sides.

Noisy Hiring Channels

Most Web3 hiring happens in chaotic group chats and social feeds, where serious talent is drowned out by spam and hype.

Low-Quality Applicants

Projects waste time filtering through people with no experience, fake portfolios, or zero intent to deliver real work.

Unclear & Unpaid Work

Job posts disappear, budgets change, and contributors are often left unpaid with no system to enforce accountability.

No Real Structure

There’s no standard way to list roles, track progress, or hold both sides accountable to timelines and payments.

Talent Gets Missed

Skilled professionals struggle to find legitimate work because opportunities are scattered across dozens of platforms.

Trust Is Broken

With no escrow or enforcement layer, both jobbers and projects hesitate to commit fully.
